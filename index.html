Screening for Left-Ventricular Dysfunction from Photographed 12-Lead ECGs

My role · Built and trained the model + the WhatsApp deployment
  • Multi-task EfficientNet-B3 ensemble predicting ejection fraction and wall-motion abnormality from a photo of a 12-lead ECG.
  • Three-stage transfer learning, inverse-density loss weighting, out-of-fold cross-validation; severe-EF AUROC 0.76.
  • Diagnosed and fixed shortcut-learning and regression-to-the-mean via Grad-CAM. Deployed as a multilingual WhatsApp triage tool.

ECG is All You Need — Cross-Modal Cardiac Inference with LLMs

My role · Co-author
  • First demonstration that LLMs can infer echocardiographic findings from a 12-lead ECG image alone — zero-shot, no fine-tuning.
  • Retrospective comparison of three LLMs against the cardiologist gold standard on 311 paired ECG–echo cases.
  • Best model reached r = 0.394 for EF with 64% of estimates within ±5%; proof-of-concept for ECG-based cardiac triage.

AI-Driven Clinical–Lifestyle Network Model for Early CVD Prediction

My role · Lead developer & first author
  • Hybrid Graph Attention Network + soft-voting ensemble predicting cardiovascular risk from questionnaire-only data — no labs or imaging.
  • Bipartite patient–risk-factor graph with a 4-head GAT learning non-linear risk-factor interactions, fused with tabular features.
  • Cross-validated AUROC 0.735 with 91.8% sensitivity at a clinical screening threshold; SHAP + attention interpretability.

About

I came to AI from biology. A biotechnology degree gave me the clinical grounding; I taught myself the machine learning, and I've spent the last year turning that combination into things people can actually use.

My focus is cross-modal medical inference and frugal AI — extracting clinical signal from cheap, ubiquitous inputs to replace expensive diagnostics where they aren't available. I care about the unglamorous engineering: cleaning messy real-world data, catching models that "cheat," staying honest about limitations, and getting a model off the screen and onto a health worker's phone.

The long-term goal is a translation layer between biological systems and computational tools — built for, and validated in, the rural Indian settings that mainstream healthcare AI tends to skip.
